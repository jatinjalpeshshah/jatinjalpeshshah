## 👋 Hi there, I'm Jatin

I am a Cybersecurity Researcher and Software Developer currently pursuing my IMCA(Integrated Master of Computer Application) with a specialization in Cyber Security & Forensics at Parul University. 

Cyber security has been my primary interest since the beginning of my academic journey. I really enjoy understanding how complex systems work, where their vulnerabilities lie, and how to build them securely from the ground up. 

Recently, my focus has naturally expanded into the intersection of security and artificial intelligence. My work mostly revolves around two main areas:
1. **Cybersecurity using AI:** Exploring how we can use AI for better threat detection, security analytics, and automating threat intelligence workflows.
2. **Cybersecurity in AI:** Looking at the security of AI systems themselves—understanding model vulnerabilities, AI risks, and how organizations can adopt these technologies securely.

## 💭 Why These Areas Interest Me

I'm fascinated by how artificial intelligence is changing both the defensive and offensive sides of cybersecurity. While AI gives us incredible new ways to detect threats and automate security operations, it also creates entirely new attack surfaces and security challenges. Trying to understand both sides of that equation—how to use AI to secure systems, while also figuring out how to secure the AI itself—is what drives most of my research and development work.

## 🚀 What I'm Exploring Right Now

I try to keep my hands dirty by building projects and analyzing data. Lately, I've been spending my time exploring:

* **AI Security & Model Risks:** Learning how machine learning pipelines can be compromised and how to prevent it.
* **Threat Intelligence Workflows:** Figuring out how to better collect and analyze data to understand what attackers are doing.
* **Security Analytics:** Using data-driven approaches to get better visibility into network health.
* **Secure Software Engineering:** Writing software where security is treated as a core feature rather than an afterthought.
